1. Detailed Project Report (To be finished by 30th May 2026)
This is the core documentation of your work. Ensure it includes the following sections with sufficient technical depth:
A. Design Documentation
	•	Solid Models & Drawings:
	◦	Fully dimensioned 2D engineering drawings (orthographic views, sections).
	◦	3D CAD models (rendered images, exploded views, assembly diagrams).
	◦	Critical design features highlighted (e.g., joints, interfaces).
	•	Material Selection & Specification:
	◦	Justification for chosen materials (e.g., mechanical properties, cost, manufacturability).
	◦	Material data sheets or references to standards (e.g., ASTM, ISO).
	•	Tolerancing & GD&T:
	◦	Tolerance analysis for critical fits/functions.
	◦	Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) symbols where applicable.
B. Analysis & Validation
	•	Simulation Results:
	◦	Stress/strain, thermal, or fluid dynamics analysis (include plots, contour maps).
	◦	Comparison with theoretical calculations or design requirements.
	•	Optimization Iterations:
	◦	Summary of design changes made based on analysis (e.g., "Increased wall thickness by 2mm to reduce stress concentrations").
	◦	Trade-off studies (e.g., weight vs. strength).
C. Supporting Documents
	•	SRR (System Requirements Review):
	◦	Original problem statement, customer needs, and functional requirements.
	•	PDR (Preliminary Design Review):
	◦	Early design concepts, selection rationale, and feasibility analysis.
	•	Manufacturability Assessment:
	◦	Production methods (e.g., machining, 3D printing) and cost estimates.
	◦	Design-for-Manufacturing (DFM) improvements.
D. Results & Conclusions
	•	Summary of key achievements and lessons learned.
	•	Recommendations for future iterations.

	•	Results & Discussion (Data interpretation, limitations).
